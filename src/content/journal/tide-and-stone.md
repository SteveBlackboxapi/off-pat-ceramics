The Tide & Stone series began with one bowl that wouldn't sit still in
my head. I'd thrown it on a Tuesday in November after a storm. The
glaze, when it came out of the kiln, looked like the beach the morning
after — sand, foam, the dark wet line where the sea had reached.

I made another. Then another. Each one a little different. The series
is still open — every piece is one-of-a-kind, fired in small batches,
salt-glazed in a wood kiln down on the headland.
